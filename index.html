Active Listening Exercise

A structured session to understand your teammates better.
Total Duration: 45 Minutes

1Preparation & Groups

Form a group of 3 or 4 students. Take a few minutes to organize yourselves and say hello.

Assign the initial roles for the first round:

  • 1 Talker
  • 1 Writer / Secretary
  • 1 or 2 Active Listeners (depending on group size)

üëÇ Active Listener(s)
  • Listen carefully without interrupting.
  • Use non-verbal cues (nodding) or brief sounds ("aha", "mmhmm") to encourage the talker.
  • Write down questions that pop into your mind.
  • If the talker stops, ask one of your questions to keep the flow going.

3Discussion Topics (For the Talker)

The Talker should address the following points during their turn:

  • Part 1: About Yourself
    • What is your name and age?
    • Where are you from?
    • What is your background in Web Development?
    • What kind of projects have you done before?
    • Do you have other skills that could benefit the group?
  • Part 2: Project Expectations
    • What would you like to eat in the evenings?
    • Where would you like to go after school?
    • Do you know any good places in Las Palmas?
    • What would you like to focus on in the project (design, coding, something else)?
    • What do you think you will be able to accomplish during this project?

4Rotation Rules

The time per person depends on your group size to fit the exercise into 45 minutes:

üë• Group of 3 People: 15 Minutes per person (45m total).

üë• Group of 4 People: 10 Minutes per person (40m total + 5m buffer).

Use the timer below to track rounds. When the alarm sounds, stop and switch roles immediately.
